# Zenify.cx

## Project Goal

Zenify.cx is being built as a leading Customer Experience Management (`CXM`) platform for businesses of every size. The system provides an end-to-end omnichannel customer experience solution that helps companies attract, serve, retain, and grow customers through one unified SaaS platform.

Zenify.cx is positioned against platforms such as `Zendesk`, `LiveChat`, and similar CX/customer support products, but with a broader full-journey approach that combines marketing touchpoints, sales enablement, support operations, AI automation, and subscription-based growth workflows.

## Product Positioning

Zenify.cx is intended to be:

- An all-in-one SaaS platform for omnichannel customer experience.
- Suitable for startups, SMEs, and enterprise customers.
- Focused on the complete customer lifecycle, not only customer support.
- Designed with modern SaaS onboarding, trial, billing, and conversion flows.

## Core SaaS Journey

The project should be structured like a complete SaaS experience:

1. Customer lands on the marketing website / landing page.
2. Customer reads the product introduction, feature highlights, use cases, and pricing value.
3. Customer signs up for a free trial account.
4. Customer is redirected to a billing step to add a payment card.
5. Customer starts a `15-day free trial`.
6. During the trial, the product demonstrates value through onboarding, usage prompts, and activation flows.
7. When the trial ends, the system prompts the customer to purchase a paid package to continue using the service.

## Pricing Direction

The package and billing experience should be designed similarly to `LiveChat.com`, with a clear SaaS pricing model that is easy to compare and upgrade.

Suggested pricing structure:

- `Starter`: for small businesses beginning with customer support and live chat.
- `Growth`: for growing teams that need automation, omnichannel inboxes, and reporting.
- `Business`: for advanced operations with AI workflows, CRM depth, and team management.
- `Enterprise`: for large organizations that need security, scale, custom integrations, and dedicated support.

Each plan can include:

- Number of seats / agents
- Channel access
- Automation limits
- AI feature limits
- Reporting and analytics depth
- CRM and integration capabilities
- Support / onboarding level

## Proposed Product Scope

Zenify.cx should evolve as a full CXM platform covering:

- Marketing touchpoints and lead capture
- Omnichannel messaging and inbox management
- Sales CRM and pipeline support
- Customer support and ticketing
- AI chatbot and AI voicebot
- Workflow automation
- Customer analytics and dashboards
- Subscription billing and plan management
- Trial lifecycle and conversion flows

## Suggested Project Structure

This project should be built around the following product areas:

- `Landing Page`: product story, trust signals, use cases, CTA, pricing, demo booking.
- `Auth & Signup`: registration, login, email verification, account creation.
- `Trial Onboarding`: workspace setup, guided onboarding, initial activation steps.
- `Billing`: card collection, subscription creation, invoicing, renewal management.
- `Application Dashboard`: the main SaaS product experience after signup.
- `Plan Upgrade Flow`: upgrade, downgrade, seat expansion, trial expiration handling.
- `Admin / Operations`: internal tools for support, customer success, and subscription oversight.

## Expected User Flow

`Landing Page -> Product Introduction -> Free Trial Signup -> Payment Method / Card Setup -> 15-Day Trial -> Trial Expiry Prompt -> Paid Subscription Upgrade`

## Long-Term Vision

Zenify.cx is not just a landing page project. It should become a scalable SaaS platform for customer experience management, with strong UX, subscription mechanics, product-led growth, and enterprise-ready expansion over time.
